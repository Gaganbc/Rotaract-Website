About Rotaract

Rotaract is a global organization that brings together young adults aged 18 and above to take action in their communities, develop leadership and professional skills, and promote international understanding and peace. Sponsored by Rotary International, Rotaract clubs are either community-based or university-based and are part of Rotary's efforts to invest in the leaders of tomorrow.
Rotaract was founded on March 13, 1968, in North Carolina, USA. The name "Rotaract" is derived from "Rotary in Action." Over the years, the movement has expanded rapidly across the globe. Today, there are over 10,000 Rotaract clubs in more than 180 countries, involving hundreds of thousands of members committed to service, fellowship, and leadership.
